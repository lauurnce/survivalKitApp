## 👩‍🎓 Persona 1 — Andrea, 18 · "The Cram-Mode Freshie"

**BSIT 1st Year · provincial state university · 🔴 PRIMARY ICP — SELL NOW**

> "Exam sa CP1 bukas 8AM. May nag-share ng link sa GC — sana may reviewer na hindi ako lolokohin."

| | |
| --- | --- |
| **Age** | 17–19 |
| **School** | State / provincial university (USTP, PLV, CatSU…) |
| **Device** | Android phone, mobile data |
| **Wallet** | GCash, allowance-funded, ₱49–99 ceiling |
| **Found via** | Section GC / Messenger link |
| **Dreams of** | Cybersecurity, IT support careers |

**Job to be done**
- **Pass CP1 tonight.** Not "learn programming" — survive tomorrow's exam. CP1 is her entire universe (52% of all site traffic).
- Find one trustworthy reviewer instead of 40 scattered GC screenshots and YouTube tabs.

**Pains & objections**
- Expects study material to be free — the free first activity has to prove value before any ask.
- No card; if checkout isn't GCash-native she's gone.
- "Legit ba 'to?" — an unknown site asking money the night before an exam triggers scam radar.

**Behavior (measured)**
- Visits once and disappears — 77% of devices are one-day-only. Monetization must land in the first session.
- Arrives in bursts around exam dates, not steadily.

**How to win her**
- Time pushes to the exam calendar; ₱49 is her impulse ceiling.
- Shareable quiz score cards — her GC is our only distribution channel.
- Sell her whole block through the class rep (₱999/subject-sem) so she never pays alone.

📎 *Evidence: 4,056 devices (74% of traffic) · CP1 2,940 devices · Intro to Computing 779 · MMW 586 · the one organic sale ever = ₱49 CP1 tier (Jul 3) · 95% no-referrer entries · profiles n=10: all BSIT, 17–20, female-skewed, provincial state universities.*

1. **Fix the teaser, not the price** — 627 students reached the paywall in a week; 608 bounced off the teaser itself. Rewrite it around Andrea's moment ("pass tomorrow's CP1 exam") and make the free first activity impossible to miss. *(Serves: Andrea · Signal: 3.0% teaser CTR)*
2. **Sell to the block, not the student** — Andrea's ₱49 ceiling caps solo revenue, but she travels in a 40-person GC. Class-rep block deals (₱999/subject-sem, ₱1,999/all) turn one conversation into a month's goal. *(Serves: Andrea, Miguel · Signal: 95% GC-shared entries)*
3. **Ride the exam calendar** — 77% of visitors give us exactly one session, clustered around exam dates. Shareable quiz score cards and exam-week pushes put the product back into the GC at the only moment that matters. *(Serves: Andrea · Signal: 77% one-day visitors)*
4. **Ship 3rd year, email the 19** — Jasmine's cohort hand-raises at 14× the freshman rate and the seed scaffolds already exist. Near-zero acquisition cost. *(Serves: Jasmine · Signal: 13% waitlist rate)*
5. **Park capstone as the premium flagship** — 54% of the waitlist needs capstone help: biggest stated demand, highest willingness to pay. Different product (templates + playbook), so it waits until the freshman engine converts. Target: next semester. *(Serves: Paolo, Jasmine · Signal: 54% needs_capstone)*

## ⚠️ Method & Caveats

- **Sources:** `events` (89,727 rows), `waitlist` (82), `profiles` (10), `payments` ledger — live Supabase, queried Jul 13, 2026. Personas are composites; names and quotes are illustrative.
- "Devices" ≠ people: one student on two devices double-counts; a shared phone undercounts.
- Registered profiles are n=10 — directional for demographics, not statistically firm.
- Paywall events only tracked since the Jul 6 analytics fix; the funnel uses that consistent window.
- Waitlist year mix is skewed by placement (79 of 82 signups came from 3rd/4th-year coming-soon pages) — it overstates upper-year share, but that's exactly why the 3rd-year hand-raise rate is meaningful.
- Owner test purchases and manual grants are excluded from sales counts (1 organic sale, ₱49).
